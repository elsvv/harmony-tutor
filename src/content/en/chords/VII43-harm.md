# VII43-harm — Leading-tone 4-3 chord in harmonic major

## How to read the label

`VII43-harm` breaks down as:

-   `VII` — the scale degree (leading tone).
-   `43` — the 4–3 figured-bass inversion of a 7th chord (second inversion): the **5th** of the chord is in the bass.
-   `-harm` — harmonic major color: the lowered 6th degree (♭6) is used, turning this sonority into a **fully diminished seventh** (°7) and sharpening its tendency tones.

## Structure

Second inversion (4-3) of the leading-tone 7th chord in harmonic major:

-   **IV** — in the bass
-   **♭VI**
-   **VII**
-   **II**

## Function

Dominant-function chord with strong tendency tones.

## Practical note

Keep resolutions stepwise; treat the lowered 6th as a high-tension color tone.
